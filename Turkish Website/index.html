Turkish Table Manners & Hospitality

Turkey is known for its warm hospitality and strong dining etiquette. Whether you're invited to a local’s home or dining at a restaurant, it’s important to understand and respect Turkish table manners.

1. Greetings & Seating

When arriving at a Turkish home, greet your host warmly. Wait for them to show you where to sit.

2. Shoes Off Indoors

It is customary to remove your shoes before entering a home. Some hosts may provide indoor slippers.

3. Respect for Elders

Elders are served first. Always wait for the host or eldest person to start eating before you begin.

4. Using Cutlery & Hands

Most dishes are eaten with cutlery, but some traditional foods like kebabs and bread can be eaten by hand.

5. Sharing & Generosity

Turkish hospitality is generous. Never refuse food too quickly—it may be considered impolite.

6. Drinking Etiquette

Tea is served after meals. If offered tea, it is polite to accept. When drinking raki (a local alcoholic beverage), toast by making eye contact.

7. Paying the Bill

In restaurants, it is common for one person to pay for the whole group. If you want to pay, insist firmly.

8. Thanking the Host

After a meal, thank the host by saying "Ellerinize sağlık" (meaning "Health to your hands").
